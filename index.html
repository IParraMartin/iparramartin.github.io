About Me

I am a Master's student in Romance Languages: Spanish linguistics at the University of Alabama. I graduated with a BA in Modern languages with a focus on English linguistics at the University of Deusto (Bilbao, Spain).

Currently my research is focused on computational linguistics and natural language processing. Inside these areas, I have studied prompt engineering, new methods to evaluate large language models' (LLMs) low-resource language (LRL) performance, and language models' biases. I am interested in the moral and ethics of AI and how to make it more inclusive and fair.

I am the founder of the Visibility Project, a project to map endangered languages and drive their NLP research. I have also made significant contributions to the AYA project by Cohere for AI.

Projects

The Visibility Project

The Visibility Project is an interactive map to locate endangered or low-resource languages (LRLs). It has a focus on natural language processing; we aim at making LRLs more visible and drag attention for their research. Each language is represented by a marker on the map, the bigger the size, the more speakers it has. The color represents the NLP attention it receives. Clicking on a marker will display a popup with information about the language. The popup also contains a link to the language's NLP papers, if available.

AYA: Cohere For AI

I collaborated in the development of AYA, an initiative to accelerate multilingual AI progress. It is part of Cohere for AI, a project to make AI more accessible. I was the ambassador and main contributor for the Basque language. As such, I was in charge of social social-media news and updates for this language and the translation of official documents of the project.
